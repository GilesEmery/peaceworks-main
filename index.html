The Problem

Every organization has two operating systems.

One drives performance. The other drives relationships. When the relational system is weak, pressure exposes it — in meetings, in trust, in leadership fatigue, and in the hidden drag that slows everything down.

Meetings where no one says the real thing.

Tension that quietly slows execution.

High performers competing instead of collaborating.

Leaders carrying emotional weight they were never trained to carry.

HR becoming the conflict department.

Relational drag that is not on the balance sheet, but felt in every room.

The result is relational drag.

Not visible on a balance sheet. But felt in every room.
